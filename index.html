Elise is a Ph.D. student in the Center for Complex Networks and Systems Research, School of Informatics and Computing, Indiana University. Her track of study is complex systems, with minor in computational linguistics. Her Ph.D. advisor is Professor Yong-Yeol Ahn, and she also works with Professor Simon Dedeo. Previous to joining the program, she received Bachelor's degree from Sun Yat-sen University in 2014 and 2015, majoring in Information Science and Anthropology. Her current research applies data science techniques, in particular natural language processing tools, to study the behaviour of people and social groups.

As hobbies, she had training in violin, parkour and drawing. Her most recent interest is traveling, and she has some travel photos here.
